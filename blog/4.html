RSS!

So last week I briefly talked about an e-mail that a reader sent me, that reader was Julian Beittel from beittel.net, and on that e-mail (among other things) was a suggestion to add an RSS feed to this blog. And I must confess that although I knew of the existence of RSS I knew nothing else about it, but after a bit of research I quickly fell in love with it and of course decided to add it (There should be a little button at the top of the page where you can get it).

If you don't know what RSS is here is a very simplified overview, RSS is literally just a simple XML file that gets parsed by some software to display posts and link to them, those pieces of software can grab a lot of RSS feeds from different sources and display the last posts in a simple to digest manner, and some of them allow you to set categories and tags to organize them.

And the best part is that a lot of modern websites like YouTube have RSS feeds for the content on them, they just don't advertise these feeds because it would mean you wouldn't have to use these websites as much, and that's why I immediately fell in love with RSS, I can just grab the feeds for the couple of channels that I want to keep up with using https://www.youtube.com/feeds/videos.xml?channel_id=[CHANNEL ID] and not be constantly tempted with suggestions by the algorithm and carefully crafted thumbnails and titles, and to top it off I can also add feeds for other cool websites like xkcd.com or countless blogs like this one, and even other streaming platforms like Nebula, all of this centralized in one simple and customizable feed reader that only updates the list of posts when I want it to (I am using Newsflash and have it setup to update the feeds on startup) so I am not constantly being bombarded with notifications that distract me!

So if you haven't already I strongly suggest that you give it a shot!
